Covid 19 : For Better or For Worse – It’s about Your Perspective

The emotional impact that novel coronavirus (COVID-19) has bought, is being dealt at individual levels; with each one of us experiencing a range of emotions. While there is undoubtedly an increase in anxiety and stress; people are also becoming more aware of their mental and emotional wellbeing. While we are reflecting on the negative impact of this disease, we are also hoping for a cure soon.

Well, 2020 has been a year no one anticipated at all. As we come to realize that ‘life’ as we knew, will not be the same; the year 2020 has also made us understand what truly is essential for us to be happy. It’s the moments spend with the family, listening to the stillness and embracing the change; no matter how hard we find it in the beginning. It’s looking forward to taking that walk around the block, going out only to put out the bin or shop for household essentials, keeping a check on maintaining that distance and merely hoping for the normality of it all to begin.

We may all be worried about our loved ones, especially those who are vulnerable; but there is also a lot of frustration and eagerness to do the ‘Normal’ at the soonest. The lockdown hasn’t been a comfortable experience, yet we cannot ignore the significant positive side of it. Yes, novel coronavirus (COVID-19) has increased anxiety and stress, and it is easy to feel despair; but it has made us more resilient than we ever thought we could be. Here are some of the most positive impact of this global pandemic:

Work-Life Balance

We have found exciting ways to work from home. Even though we sometimes juggle with too many online meetings; the work-life balance has never been so flexible! What was once considered unprofessional is now quite common and accepted. People are indeed becoming more tolerant and patient. Just hoping this positive change continues once the workplace opens back.

Massive Transformation in Education

With online classes, self-led learning, and so much more, home-schooling has been crazy. But; the kids too are learning the virtual way of education and are now much more comfortable and confident in handling their daily tasks as compared to the initial struggle with online learning techniques. Photo by Julia M Cameron from Pexels PIC 3 As parents, we too were given a rare chance to support our kids with their school online learning, and I feel both of us have adapted well and learnt so much more than we could in normal circumstances. Now we know how much kids are doing at school! Our respect for teachers too has grown in leaps. God bless them!

Better Sense of Community and Connectedness

Even though, covid-19 has restricted our travels or meeting with our loved ones; never have we felt more concerned and connected with friends and family. People across communities are coming forward to support those in need of food, healthcare or necessities. There’s a sense of community belonging and companies too are donating towards affected people and offering support.

Innovation in Home-Delivery

From a quick takeaway from your favourite local café to ordering those handpicked bottles of wine/beer or getting special food delivery from your favourite place in the city, the venues and businesses are reinventing unique ways to bring their services at your doorstep.

Nature has switched its RESET button

Even during the darkest of storms, there’s a silver lining. With Covid-19’s setback, our environment has got an opportunity to heal and reset. There is a significant improvement in air quality, and with less traffic on the streets, our pollution levels have also declined. Across the world, people are talking about watching the wildlife coming out in the open, claiming their land and have seen nature thriving in all its glory. Now our learning should be towards innovative ways for clean energy and treating the environment with care.

We are More Grateful than Ever

Our perspective towards life indeed has changed this past few months, and we surely have more appreciation and gratitude towards everything around us. The sense of being grateful for our freedom, the joy of meeting our loved ones, planning that holiday trip or only going shopping or watching a movie makes us realize how much given up these affect us. As heartbreaking it is to lose people we loved to this global crisis, count your blessings to be resilient enough to come out of these times with gratitude. We are also thankful and grateful for the amazing support of all those at the frontline, the doctors, the nurses and all those who held fort 24/7 for our health & safety. With restrictions in place and routines reduced, we may get a bit frustrated, but this phase too shall pass. Remember, time is constant, and it will change. Covid pandemic may have changed us and our world for now, but that is a positive change. Hopefully, it will make us better humans, empathetic individuals and kinder towards each other.
