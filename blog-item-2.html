Let’s Talk about Mental Health

Talking about or around Mental Health has never been easy. There’s always a bit of hesitation and doubt. People are unsure about responding to someone who is talking about their mental wellbeing or get uncomfortable while discussing their feelings. Most also don’t understand ‘Mental Health’ or consider it as a mere ‘mood swing’ phase.

The whole mindset needs to be changed, and the only way we can do that is by talking on Mental Health. We need to create opportunities for people first to understand what Mental Health is, what all it constitutes, including kind of Mental Health issues and knowing how to deal with it. With every conversation we have with our family or friends on mental health, we will be able to come out more confident and less embarrassed about it.

Consider these steps and talk about your mental health and combat it without fear:

Keep The Talk Informal

While discussing your mental and emotional health, you need not have to explain it in big words. Its not going to be easy at first but start by sharing how you currently feel. You may lack motivation, feel dull most of the day or have the heavy feeling in your chest and the whirlwind of never-ending sad thoughts or emotions.

No matter what, talk about your emotions and feelings clearly and define them as much as you comfortably can. Your family or friends may at first find it a bit confusing, but they will understand. Not speaking about your mental health can never help you or people around you. Talking about it can remarkably change your lives and of those around you.

Maintain Meaningful Relations

Find someone you trust and can be open about speaking your heart out. Living with anxiety is challenging and can make you feel vulnerable to share your feelings easily. Just reflect on your emotions and focus on your achievements and let the thoughts flow. Trusted friends and family will not judge you and will encourage you at every step in your road to recovery, slowly but steadily. The friends & family we choose, hold the key to our overall wellbeing! Choose Wisely and Nurture them.

While it is vital to start discussing your mental and emotional health regularly; only do so if you are comfortable with it. And yes, try to be open to suggestions. Sometimes simple advice can change your current life for the better. With care and time, you will soon be to your road to recovery.
