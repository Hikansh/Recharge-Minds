Find Your Calm – RESET, REBALANCE & REFRESH

Rest is not just 'sleeping'; it can be of so many types. Choose what you feel you need:

Physical: Getting into a sleep routine can benefit your physical body. Not only does adequate sleep help in slowing down our thoughts; it also gives body required time to repair and energize.

Mental: No matter how busy your lifestyle is, ensure that you take some time off to disconnect and connect to the present moment and enjoy the stillness. Simply being mindful of every moment can offer you enhanced focus and will benefit your overall health.

To be happy, forget what's gone, appreciate what remains and look forward to what's coming next.

Emotional: It's hard, but try to speak about your emotions and feelings. Try to become self-aware of your thoughts and understand that you won't feel the same way all the time. Bottling up those feelings can have adverse effects on your mental and emotional health. Track your moods & behaviours and then take a moment to understand what's bringing them on. Be your authentic selves and don't bother to compare yourself with others or care much about how people see you. Remember, self-awareness takes time, but once you know what to do, you will be able to increase your emotional intelligence and conquer all your fears.

Don't keep allowing the same things to upset you. Life's too short to live that way

— Joel Osteen

Social: Life is busy, lockdown or not. Avoid burnout. Try to schedule some time off from your social calendar. Spending some quiet time for yourself can be wonderful.

Creative: Pick up something you like. It could be a creative art, playing a musical instrument or a sport. Get inspired by things around you. Getting creative creates calm, making you feel good.

Days begin with hopes and ends with dreams. Every day starts with some expectations but surely ends with some experience. That's life!

Spiritual: Our life is full of spiritual qualities or values; we only have to find it. Start by taking charge of your health & wellbeing and get attuned to your inner being. Embrace 'ONENESS'. Find your 'reason for being' by connecting to the high energy power. Being spiritual is being balanced and in the harmony of your thoughts. Compassion, empathy and open-heartedness are some virtues associated with spirituality and needs a high level of self-knowledge. It includes our beliefs, how we do things, how we interact or act in specific ways, and most importantly, knowledge of our interdependence.

Developing inner values is like physical exercise. The more we train our abilities, the stronger they become. The difference is that unlike the body when it comes to training the mind, there is no limit how far we can go

— Dalai Lama

Practices like meditation, self-reflection and (in some cases) psychotherapy can help you achieve the required balance and become more sensitive to the emotions of others, and even to one's surrounding environments, both social and natural. Be kind and show gratitude. Remember, each of us is fighting our own battles, and all we need to do right is to treat everyone with kindness and don't' judge.
